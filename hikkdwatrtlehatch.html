Hikkaduwa Hatchery

About Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Welcome to Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka, a captivating sanctuary nestled along the stunning coastline of Hikkaduwa. This renowned hatchery is a haven for sea turtles and stands as a shining symbol of dedication to their conservation and welfare. With its picturesque location and unwavering commitment, Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery has become a prominent institution in Sri Lanka's efforts to protect these magnificent creatures. The hatchery serves as a vital refuge where sea turtles can safely nest, and their precious eggs are carefully incubated until the adorable hatchlings emerge. Moreover, the hatchery actively engages in rescue and rehabilitation, providing a lifeline for injured or distressed sea turtles. Through its educational programs and immersive experiences, visitors are not only enchanted by the sheer beauty of these ancient marine beings but also gain a deeper understanding of the importance of their preservation. Join us on a remarkable journey as we explore the wonders of sea turtle conservation and witness firsthand the remarkable work taking place at Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery.

Conservation efforts

Hikkaduwa Hatchery is a remarkable conservation initiative situated in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka. With a primary focus on protecting and preserving sea turtles, the hatchery has played a crucial role in the conservation of these magnificent creatures. The dedicated team at Hikkaduwa Hatchery works tirelessly to monitor and safeguard sea turtle nests along the pristine beaches. By carefully collecting and relocating the eggs to protected areas, they ensure a higher survival rate for the hatchlings. The hatchery also conducts educational programs and awareness campaigns to promote responsible tourism and highlight the importance of sea turtle conservation. Their efforts have not only helped increase the number of hatchlings but have also contributed significantly to the overall conservation of sea turtles in the region. Hikkaduwa Hatchery stands as a shining example of successful community-based conservation, inspiring locals and visitors alike to actively participate in preserving these beautiful marine creatures for generations to come.
